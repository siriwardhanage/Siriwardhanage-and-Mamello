Dr. Norman Borlaug!

Dr. Norman Borlaug in Mexico, examining wheat research.

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist and humanitarian who led initiatives to combat world hunger. He is often called the "Father of the Green Revolution" for his contributions to the development of high-yielding wheat varieties.

Fun Facts about Dr. Norman Borlaug:

  • Dr. Borlaug was born on March 25, 1914, in Cresco, Iowa, and grew up on a small farm.
  • He earned a Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota in 1942.
  • He spent much of his career working in Mexico, where he developed new strains of disease-resistant, high-yielding wheat.
  • Dr. Borlaug is one of only seven people to have won the Nobel Peace Prize, the Presidential Medal of Freedom, and the Congressional Gold Medal.
  • He received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his work in developing high-yielding wheat varieties.
  • Dr. Borlaug once said, "You can't build a peaceful world on empty stomachs and human misery."
  • He continued working well into his 90s and died on September 12, 2009, at the age of 95.
  • Dr. Borlaug's work is estimated to have saved over a billion lives.
